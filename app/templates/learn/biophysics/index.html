Biophysics

Walking

Newton's first law states an object is at rest if no external forces are applied. Newton's third law states every force applied has an equal and opposite force applied back on the applying object (note that this is not talking about weight and normal force because those are applied to the same object whereas third law talks about two objects applying forces on each other). Walking is caused by the foot applying a backwards movement onto the ground, which causes a forward friction force moving the body forward.
