Innovation

The scientific innovation of this work includes:

  • Studies to define the pattern for DRG neuronal re-modeling in response to injury-induced IVD degeneration, and at multiple timepoints;
  • Studies to reveal the pattern for changes in neuronal function with time from acute to chronic IVD degeneration, and involvement of a broad set of sodium and other ion channels in regulating this remodeling response with IVD degeneration.

Our proposed work will utilize these methodological innovations:

  • In vivo imaging of lumbar DRGs following injury-induced IVD degeneration at acute and chronic timepoints, and defining relationships to in vivo awake animal behavior and sensitivity;
  • Imaging of lumbar DRGs in organotypic culture to measure action potential generation, its dependence on sodium channels (Nav1.5 - Nav1.8) and its remodeling with LDP-induced IVD degeneration;
  • Multi-modal longitudinal monitoring of pain-related behavior and sensitivity, DRG and IVD structural and molecular change on a subject-specific basis.

Completion of these aims will reveal new knowledge about neuronal changes that contribute to pain transmission in the development of IVD degeneration. This knowledge of in vivo “cross-talk” between DRG neurons and the IVD will be the first of its kind available, and has potential to identify novel targets for chronic disc-related pain.
